General Terms

This defines some of the more basic terms used in the Biological Anthropology exercises, for indicating locations and features.
Anterior
Toward the front; the opposite of posterior
Distal
Away from the point of attachment or origin or a central point especially of the body; the opposite of proximal
Lateral
Toward the side or outside; the opposite of medial
Medial
Toward or at the middle or midline; the opposite of lateral
Posterior
Toward the rear; the opposite of anterior
Proximal
Next to or closer to the point of attachment or origin or a central point especially of the body; the opposite of distal
Sagittal
Along the midline of the body. Example: sagittal crest, a bony prominence on top of the skull that runs front to back and sits along the midline of the skull.
