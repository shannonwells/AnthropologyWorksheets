CRANIAL TERMS

Upper facial height
Measured as the linear distance between midline landmarks nasion and prosthion (just above upper central incisor crowns), excluding the lower jaw.
Face width
Distance (mm) between left and right zygomatic (Zy), the most prominent point of the zygomatic bone.
Cranial length (antero-posterior)
Distance (mm) between G (glabella) and Op (Opisthocranion, the furthest point from glabella in the sagittal plane).
Cranial width
Distance (mm) between left and right Eurion (Eu), the most lateral points of the neurocranium.
Location of Max Skull Breadth
Divide A (height of cranial width from bottom of cranium) by B (total cranial height).
Supraorbital width
Width of supraorbital arch, from leftmost to rightmost margin.
Postorbital constriction
Width of skull past the supraorbital torus, from leftmost to rightmost margin.
Prognathism
Measured by the craniofacial angle (Frankfort horizontal vs. sellion-prosthion line). Acute in humans, obtuse in great apes.

CRANIAL REFERENCE POINTS

Sella (S)
the center of the hypophyseal fossa (sella tursica)
Nasion (N)
the junction of the nasal and frontal bones at the most posterior point on the curvature of the bridge of the nose
A-point (A)
an arbitrary measure point on the innermost curvature from the maxillary anterior nasal spine to the crest of the maxillary alveolar process. Ab is the most anterior point of the maxillary apical base.
B-point (B)
An arbitrary measure point on the anterior bony curvature of the mandible. B is the innermost curvature from chin to alveolar junction
Pogonion (Pg)
the most anterior point on the contour of the chin
Gnathion (Gn)
the most outward and everted point on the profile curvature of the symphysis of the mandible, located midway between pogonion and menton.
Menton (Me)
the lowest point on the symphysis of the mandible
Orbitale (Or)
a point midway between the lowest point on the inferior margin of the two orbits
Gonion (Go)
a point midway between the points representing the middle of the curvature at the left and right angles of the mandible
Porion (P)
the midpoint of the upper contour of the external auditory canal (Anatomic Porion) or a point midway between the top of the image of the left and right ear-rods of the cephalostat (Machine Porion)

CRANIAL REFERENCE PLANES

Sella-Nasion (S-N)
a line connecting Sella to Nasion
Frankfort Horizontal (FH)
a line connecting Porion to Orbitale
Mandibular Plane (MP)
a line connecting Go to Me
Y-axis (Y)
a line connecting Se to Pg
Upper Anterior Facial Height (UAFH)
a line connecting Na to ANS
Lower Anterior Facial Height (LAFH)
a line connecting ANS to Me
Nasion-A point (Na-A)
a line connecting Na to A
Nasion-B point (Na-B)
a line connecting Na to B
Upper Incisor (U1)
a line connecting the incisal edge and the root apex of the most prominent maxillary incisor
Lower Incisor (L1)
a line connecting the incisal edge and the root apex of the most prominent lower incisor

CRANIAL MEASUREMENTS

Nasion-Prosthion Height (n-pr, NPH)
The distance from nasion (n) to prosthion (pr), upper facial height.
Foramen Magnum Length (ba-o, FOL)
The mid-sagittal distance from the most anterior point on the foramen magnum margin to opisthion (o).
Foramen Magnum Breadth (FOB)
The distance between the lateral margins of the foramen magnum at the point of greatest lateral curvature.
Upper Facial Breadth (fmt-fmt)
The distance between the right and left frontomalare temporale (fmt).
