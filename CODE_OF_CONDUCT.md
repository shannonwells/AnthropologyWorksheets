# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Pledge
Contributors shall be committed to fostering an environment that respects and promotes the dignity, rights, and contributions of all individuals. The privilege of participation is extended to everyone who participates in earnestness and good faith and in accordance with this Code of Conduct.

Contributors shall treat others with courtesy and dignity. Contributors acknowledge that words and actions may be interpreted differently than intended based on culture, background, or native language. Contributors agree to behave decently toward each other. Call it The Golden Rule, Hillel's Maxim, Wil Wheaton's Rule, or quote Bill and Ted's Excellent Adventure, whatever you like, but this includes:

1. Respect for the **educational purpose** of this repository.
2. **Kindess**
3. **Honesty**
4. **Courtesy**
4. **Taking responsibility** for one's actions and contributions.
5. Giving and accepting **constructive feedback**.
6. **Repairing harm** when it occurs, regardless of intent, which may include apologizing, clearing up a misunderstanding and making amends.

## Restricted Behaviors
For those needing to spell it out some more, behaving decently _excludes_:

1. **Harassment.** Crossing explicitly expressed boundaries or engaging in unnecessary personal attention after any clear request to stop. That includes excessively/unnecessarily tagging your admin (@shannonwells).
2. **Character attacks.** Insulting, demeaning, or pejorative comments specifically directed at one or more people.
3. **Bigotry.** Look it up in the dictionary.
4. **Sexualization.** There are appopriate times and a places for sexualized language and imagery, and it requires consent of all involved.  This repository is neither and does not meet that requirement.
5. **Violating confidentiality**. Sharing any PII whatsoever or any other private details about someone without their express consent.
6. **Endangerment.** Causing, advocating, stating a desire for, or threatening harm toward one or more persons.
7. **Boundary testing.** Engaging in behaviors that violate the spirit but not a narrowly defined version of this Code of Conduct, such as interpretations based on obscure dictionary definitions, sarcasm, engaging in violations and then declaring one was joking when called out, etc. Boundary testing behaviors are a precursor to worse and will result in an immediate and permanent ban.

### Other requirements for posted content
Additionally, contributors shall:
1. **Represent themselves only**
2. **Credit sources.**
3. **Label AI generated content as such**
4. **Label content accurately**, for example, links are accurately described.

## Reporting an Issue
Misunderstandings happen. Tensions can occur between contributors even when they are trying their best to collaborate. Not every conflict represents a code of conduct violation, and this Code of Conduct calls for behaviors and norms that can help avoid conflicts and minimize harm.

When an incident does occur, generally try to resolve it among yourselves; if you deem it a clear and serious violation of the Code of Conduct, or your reasonable attempt to reconcile and resolve fails, mark the issue or PR with the "code of conduct" label and/or tag me (@shannonwells) so I can filter it and decide what to do.

## Addressing and Repairing Harm

As this repository's only admin, I don't have time or patience to go through any kind of "consequence" ladder. Violations of this Code of Conduct are based on my personal, culturally-biased, subjective judgment; I will ban you if I think what you are posting is excessively in violation.  Otherwise, I'll ask you to 1) apologize and 2) clarify if there was just a misunderstanding, and if you don't do those things you will be blocked. So you can either respond politely to someone's complaint about your behavior and clear it up before I see it, or you can _not_ do that, and risk being banned.

Conversely, if a contributor displays a pattern of interpreting comments as personal or bigoted attacks (such as, any time someone disagrees, regardless of how they do so), I may ban them. It is outside the bounds of this repository for contributors to perform psychoanalysis or political activism.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all areas of contribution in this repository.
